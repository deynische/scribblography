Figuring the Basics of Design

12 Dec 2018 ｜ 3 min read
design

No matter what skill, you always want a good handle on your basics. It’s the same with design.

If you ever try looking up the basics of design, you’ll find all sorts of lists. Here are the 5 principles of design. Here are the 10 elements of design. Here are the 8 basics of design. So… which guide do you read?

Well the problem is that everyone is conflating three modes of thought:

  • The elements of design
  • The principles of design
  • The Gestalt design principles

Yes, they all unfortunately sound very similar. Even their contents appear nearly identical. But trust me, they are all distinct from one another.

It’s easier to think them as three parts of design:

  • The components
  • The methodology
  • The philosophy

Or in simpler language:

  • The parts
  • How to use the parts
  • Why we use the parts that way

A popular format for any guide is as follows: introduce an idea, instruct implementation, and conclude with an explanation of effectiveness. Unfortunately, moving between the elements to the principles of design is not so linear. For each design element, you can apply anywhere from one or all of the design principles. Of course, each combination can also utilize a Gestalt design principle. As you can see, there is no linear progression when it comes to explaining design. It’s multiplicative.

We naturally learn design in an organically disorganized way. We refine line techniques that give the illusion of shape and texture. We intuit how to illicit emotions without fully understanding the mechanisms behind it. In the creative space, we seamlessly flow and flitter from one mishmash of thoughts to another. But when confronted to explain the basics, we might have an inkling of what we do but we’ve never exactly put words to it.

Think of frying an egg. You understand the motions to a point where you can see, smell, hear, and taste them. But have you ever had to explain exactly why a fried egg is delicious?

Similar deal.

For my own practice putting the basics of design into words, I’ll be covering over each part in their own respective articles. While there are a multitude of authorities on these subjects, my goal is to keep everything as simple as possible.
